How to choose the right niacinamide serum

Start with your skin type and main concern. Niacinamide is flexible, but not every formula is built for every face.

  • Very oily / acne-prone: look at The Ordinary, COSRX 15% or Anua 10% + TXA.
  • Uneven tone & dark spots: La Roche-Posay Mela B3, Anua, Beauty of Joseon.
  • Dry or barrier-damaged: CeraVe Night Cream or a lower-strength moisturiser.
  • Just want a simple start: The Inkey List or The Ordinary at 10% used slowly.

What % do you actually need?

  • 2–5%: gentle, great for sensitive or redness-prone skin.
  • 5–10%: the sweet spot for most people and most concerns.
  • 15–20%: niche territory – treat as a targeted treatment, not a casual daily step.

If your face feels hot, itchy or tight, it’s not a sign that the serum is “working harder”. It usually means your barrier wants a break.

Simple routine examples with niacinamide

1. Oily / acne-prone, beginner

  1. Gentle foaming cleanser.
  2. Niacinamide 10% serum 2–3 nights per week (The Ordinary or Inkey List).
  3. Light, non-comedogenic moisturiser.
  4. SPF 30+ every morning.

2. Redness + dark spots

  1. Gentle cream cleanser.
  2. Niacinamide brightening serum (Mela B3, Anua or Beauty of Joseon).
  3. Barrier-supporting moisturiser.
  4. Sunscreen every single day – non-negotiable.

3. Already using retinoids or acids

Use niacinamide on alternate nights or in the morning. The goal is to support your barrier, not to stack as many actives as possible.
